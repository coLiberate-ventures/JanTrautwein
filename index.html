The Reality

The AI space is one of the most exciting places to be building right now.

It's also one of the most demanding.

New models, new tools, new competitors, new use cases - every week. The culture here rewards speed and celebrates the ones who move fastest.

Most people just keep up. Or try to.

85% of founders experience high stress. 76% report feeling lonely. In the AI space, those numbers probably run higher.

There's no natural pause point, no milestone that feels stable enough to stop and ask how you're doing. With so much demand and opportunity it's easy to fall into a reactive pattern.

Some try to do too much at once. Others get so overwhelmed they can't move at all.

The people who thrive long-term aren't the ones who moved fastest. They're the ones who knew what they were actually building, and why, and could hold that steady when everything around them kept shifting.

How I Work

Deep, focused, 1:1.

I only work 1:1, no group programs. I support only a small number of clients at any given time. We go deep and far together, over several months, with bi-weekly sessions and support in between.

The work is holistic by design. We look at the whole picture — your business, your mindset, your energy, your patterns, your vision. Strategy without inner work only goes so far and inner work without practical direction stays theoretical.

Sessions happen over video call or over audio while we're both outside walking in nature. The best conversations don't always happen sitting down.

I'm selective about who I work with. When I work with someone, I'm all in to support them and their mission and their life, so I choose my clients as carefully as they choose me.

A Holistic Approach to Growth

It's both and.

Building in the AI space means your attention is naturally pulled outward - new tools, new opportunities, new decisions every week. That matters, and you'll have my full support there. But lasting progress requires looking at the full picture - your business, your mindset, your patterns, your vision.

We move between the following areas as needed. Some weeks it's strategy and priorities. Others it's the deeper internal work.

Vision

Most founders are so deep in the day-to-day that they've lost sight of what they're actually building and why. We start by getting radically clear on your vision. Not just the business goals, but the life you want to be living while you build it. Clarity at this level is the foundation.

Strategy

We turn your vision into a focused, realistic strategy that works with your energy and strengths, not against them. You'll always know what to focus on next, and you'll make consistent progress one step after another.

Overcome Resistance

This is where most growth actually happens. Every founder has patterns — avoidance, procrastination, self-doubt — that quietly slow everything down. We work on these in real time, week after week, so they stop disrupting your momentum and keeping you from the progress you're capable of.

This includes accountability — checking in week after week so you stay on track, don't get distracted, and the things that really matter actually get done.

Meaning Making

The stories we tell ourselves shape the experience we have. The meaning we attach to what happens in our business — with cofounders, investors, employees — drives how we feel and how we respond. This work is about becoming aware of those stories and learning to relate to them differently.

Identity

When we want to get to a new level in our business, that usually comes with a shift in identity. Maybe it's learning to delegate, stepping back from micromanaging, or letting others step into leadership. When our identity shifts, our behavior and actions naturally follow — rather than trying to force different results while staying the same person.
